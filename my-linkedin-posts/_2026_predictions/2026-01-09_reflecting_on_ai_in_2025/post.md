𝗠𝗲𝗮𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴𝗳𝘂𝗹 𝗟𝗟𝗠 𝗥𝗲𝗹𝗲𝗮𝘀𝗲𝘀
Looking back, 2025 was the year the "reasoning" era truly took over. Here is the timeline of how the landscape shifted:
🗓️ Jan 20 – DeepSeek R1
The first open-weights “reasoning” model. It crashed the “only the USA can build SOTA” narrative and proved how much room we still have to improve efficiency in training and inference.
🗓️ Feb 27 – GPT-4.5 “Orion”
The last non-reasoning model from OpenAI. It was huge, expensive, and slow—it never actually made it out of research preview.
🗓️ Apr 7 – Llama 4
A surprising failure. Despite Meta spending billions on compute and talent, the model flopped in a hyper-competitive market.
🗓️ Apr 15 – Qwen 3
Outperformed Llama 4 on code and logic. It created a compliance headache: it was the best open model, but of Chinese origin.
🗓️ May 22 – Claude 4 Sonnet & Opus
Sonnet 4 became the global standard for coding agents. Opus 4 introduced Extended Thinking, allowing for the generation of entire software modules in one pass.
🗓️ Aug 7 – GPT-5
A solid model that brought the GPT-5 auto-router, which intelligently switches between Main and Mini models to save on reasoning costs.
🗓️ Oct 22 – Gemini 3 Pro
Astonishing performance in spatial reasoning, powered by Google’s own TPU-v6.
🗓️ Nov 24 – Claude Opus 4.5
The current reigning champ on SWE-bench (60% pass rate). A total game-changer for agentic coding.
𝗞𝗲𝘆 𝗜𝗻𝘀𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁𝘀 𝗳𝗿𝗼𝗺 𝟮𝟬𝟮𝟱:
1️⃣ Reasoning > Speed: We stopped asking for instant answers and accepted a 10-second “think” period for better results.
2️⃣ Open-source became Chinese-first: DeepSeek R1 and Qwen 3 dominated coding benchmarks all year.
3️⃣ Agentic Workflows: In production, we moved from simple prompts to fully integrated, automated systems.
4️⃣ The Rise of Small Models: By the end of 2025, "Mini" models were outperforming the massive flagship models from the start of the year.
5️⃣ Agentic Coding: This remains the most valuable use case for LLMs, which is why Anthropic emerged as a major winner.
